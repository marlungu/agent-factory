# 04 · Human Gates

A factory runs on its own — except at the points where your judgment matters. Those points are **human gates**. Getting them right is what keeps you in control without making you babysit every step.

## What a gate is

A gate is a stop. The factory pauses, shows you what it has produced, and waits. Nothing continues until you approve. If you want a change, the agent re-runs with your feedback.

## Where to put gates

Put a gate wherever a wrong direction would be **expensive to discover late**.

The strongest gate is right after the **plan**, before any building starts. A wrong plan caught here costs one quick revision. The same wrong plan caught after everything is built costs you every downstream file. Catch it now, not after ten things have been made on top of it.

A good second gate is after the **main build**, before anything that depends on it gets made. And a final gate after **validation**, so you see the honest assessment before the work is done.

In the example factory, the three gates are:

1. After the plan (brief) — approve the read on the job before any rewriting.
2. After the main build (resume) — approve it before the cover letter is written from it.
3. After validation (evaluation) — review the fit score before the package is final.

## Where NOT to put gates

Do not gate every step. A factory that stops after every agent is just a slow manual process with extra steps. You will stop reading carefully, start rubber-stamping, and the gates become noise.

Gate the decisions. Let the mechanical steps run.

The test for whether a step needs a gate: *if this goes the wrong way, how expensive is it to find out later?* High cost, gate it. Low cost, let it run.

## What you are actually deciding at a gate

A gate is not "is this perfect?" It is one of three questions:

- **Is this the right problem?** (after research/plan)
- **Is this the right work?** (after build)
- **Is this safe to ship?** (after validation)

Those are judgment calls a human should own. Everything between them is execution the agents can handle.

## The point

The factory does not remove you from the process. It removes you from the parts that do not need you, and keeps you in the loop exactly where your judgment changes the outcome.
